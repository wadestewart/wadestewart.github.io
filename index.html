My name is Wade.

I'm a Full Stack

Web Developer.

Experience

A creative thinker who seeks an efficient work flow. I stay engaged until the solution is identified and implemented. Organization and keeping the team smiling until we cross the finish line is the name of the game as far I am concerned.

After graduating Virginia Commonwealth University, I was a Supervisor in a warehouse where I learned to work on and manage a team. From there I was an Operations Manager for a nonprofit and gained experience in volunteer coordination and computer hardware refurbishment. I have always been passionate about art and design, so during this period I picked up a gig as a Wordpress Designer/Editor and began my journey into web development. I have spent the past year honing my coding skills through General Assembly’s web development immersive program, freelance and personal projects.

What is your favorite analog product?

A pencil

It is the beginning of the creative process. I draw as much as possible, so the pencil is my creative outlet on a personal level. Wire-framing represents the beginning of the creative process for web development. The more in depth we get when planning a build, the less bugs we encounter when building out.

What’s the best advice you’ve heard recently?

Engage daily in your craft, to build muscle memory

During a talk at Chief (an advertising agency in Washington, DC). The speaker was referring to Graham Greene's methodical writing regimen to hone his craft. It resonated with me because the daily commitment to getting better applies to many areas in life, not just skill development.

What do you like to do when not designing or coding?

Golf

To call it exercise would be a stretch, but I do walk when I play. I enjoy the challenge to both mind and body. Golf is not intuitive, it takes a lot of practice. I enjoy the slow pace, being outside, and socializing. The challenge is more a personal one, meaning an individual challenge that I must overcome to remain focused. The same could said for coding.
